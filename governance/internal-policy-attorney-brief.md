# Internal Policy & Attorney Brief (v1.0)
*Binding internal rules + the attorney session packet. Every rule carries its
reason so nobody "optimizes" it away. Exceptions require written founder
sign-off (Part G).*

## PART A — Marketing Claims Policy ("Never Say")
Applies to founder, staff, contractors, affiliates, influencers.
A1. Never: specific income/revenue as expected outcomes ("make $X/mo," "six
figures," "replace your salary/9–5," "quit your job").
A2. Banned vocabulary: passive income, guaranteed, proven system, risk-free,
can't fail, autopilot profits, money while you sleep, financial freedom,
fire your boss, get rich.
A3. Testimonials: must be real + records retained; disclose material
connections; show typical results adjacent, equal type ("Most users do not
achieve results like these; median active store earned $X; N% closed").
A4. AI claims: only what we can substantiate with retained records. Allowed:
factual descriptions. Banned: "AI guarantees winners," unprovable accuracy.
A5. No fabricated credibility (fake press, fake user counts, fake
partnerships). No lifestyle-as-result imagery. No desperation targeting
(debt, unemployment, medical bills themes).
A6. Substantiation file: any performance claim gets its data/method/date
saved BEFORE it ships. Can't file it → can't say it.
A7. Affiliates: contracts incorporate A1–A5, require FTC disclosure in every
post, 48h takedown right, termination + clawback; monthly spot-checks
logged. (FTC treated affiliate claims as the company's own.)
A8. Reviews of us: no gag clauses anywhere (CRFA); never buy/seed reviews.
*Reason: active FTC sweep in exactly this market — Automators AI ($21.7M,
lifetime bans), Ascend Ecom ($25M+), Click Profit, Ecommerce Empire
Builders, Ecom Genie (~$14M).*

## PART B — Never Build (each: why → instead)
B1 Fake countdown/sale timers → real deadlines only.
B2 Fake stock scarcity → real supplier stock via API.
B3 Fake/purchased/imported reviews or star fabrication → real post-purchase
review flows.
B4 AI "customer testimonial" content posing as real buyers (FTC fake-review
rule) → ad/demonstration framing only; templates locked.
B5 Fictional social-proof popups → real order events only.
B6 Review gating → uniform requests + service recovery.
B7 Processor-evasion tooling (descriptor games, new-account-after-ban help,
volume spreading) → dispute PREVENTION suite only.
B8 Ad cloaking → ad-policy pre-check that helps comply.
B9 Auto-spend without hard caps → execution-layer caps + confirmations.
B10 "Exclusive picks" sold to unlimited users → disclosed distribution.
B11 Cancellation mazes, hidden auto-renew, credit obfuscation → two-click
cancel, pre-renewal emails, flat pricing.
B12 SMS/email without consent capture (TCPA/CAN-SPAM) → built-in consent
records, one-click unsubscribe.
B13 Scraping behind logins / spoofed accounts → public data, official APIs,
licensed vendors.
B14 Auto-listing regulated products unflagged → Part C guardrails.
B15 Health/medical claims in generated copy → hard blocklist.
B16 Selling/exploiting individual user data; operating competing stores →
aggregated opt-in benchmarks only (Part D).

## PART C — Always Build
C1 Automation safety: caps in the execution layer; per-action audit log
(≥24 months); global + per-agent kill switch; default human-approves;
re-confirm on cap changes.
C2 Category guardrails (flag or exclude): children's products, mains/lithium
electrical, ingestibles/supplements/cosmetics, medical-adjacent devices,
weapons/age-restricted, counterfeit/branded-IP matches, recalls; EU sales →
GPSR responsible-person warning; forced-labor sourcing risk flag (UFLPA —
customs can seize goods).
C3 Creative engine: health-claims blocklist; required ad disclosures; no
testimonial framing; platform-policy pre-check.
C4 Money-adjacent: tax info via licensed engines with "informational only —
consult a professional"; any financing via licensed partners; payment
features framed/built as compliance improvement only.
C5 Access: 18+ gate; sanctions/geo screening; clean wind-down flow
(Charter §7).

## PART D — Data Ethics
D1 No front-running: company/staff/contractors may not run consumer retail
stores in covered categories; annual attestation.
D2 Store-level data never displayed to others, sold, or licensed; support
access logged.
D3 Benchmarks: ≥50 stores per cell, k-anonymity check, opt-in, no penalty
for opting out.
D4 Pick saturation tracked and published; "exclusive" banned unless
code-enforced.
D5 Model training: aggregated signals only; never strategy-identifying
content without consent.
D6 Deletion within 30 days except legally required records.

## PART E — Attorney Session Agenda
E1 Business Opportunity Rule (16 CFR 437) exposure: confirm self-serve SaaS
+ our marketing stays outside it; flag bright lines (coaching features,
any future done-for-you tier).
E2 ToS review (Part F): liability cap, arbitration/class waiver yes-or-no,
financial-advice disclaimer, automation consent, indemnification.
E3 Charter-in-ToS: avoid unintended warranty liability; reconcile with
disclaimers.
E4 Data source list review: ToS-breach/CFAA exposure per source; vendor
contract terms.
E5 Privacy program: policy, GDPR/UK/CCPA applicability, DPA template (we
process users' customers' data), breach plan.
E6 Affiliate agreement per A7.
E7 Auto-renewal compliance across states (and EU if sold there).
E8 Entity + insurance: LLC state, founder hygiene, E&O/cyber timing.
E9 Future outcome-based pricing: confirm it doesn't recharacterize us.
E10 AI content: confirm B4/C3 satisfies the FTC fake-review rule + platform
AI-disclosure policies.

## PART F — ToS Clause Skeleton (counsel formalizes)
Software/information only; no results guaranteed; not financial/legal/tax
advice; user is merchant of record and responsible for products/claims/
compliance • automation consent: user-set rules/caps, our anomaly-suspend
right • honest billing terms incl. two-click cancel + renewal notice •
Part D data terms in binding form • acceptable use (no deception,
counterfeits, restricted categories) • charter incorporation per E3 •
liability cap/indemnity/arbitration per counsel • zero gag clauses.

## PART G — Enforcement
Quarterly review + "charter check" on every feature spec. Exceptions need
written founder sign-off with reasoning, stored permanently. New hires read
Parts A, B, D on day one and sign.
